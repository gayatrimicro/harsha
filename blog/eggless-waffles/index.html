Delight yourself with this simple eggless waffles

Want to start your morning off with a great breakfast that is crispy and crunchy on the exterior, fluffy and buttery in its interior, of high nourishing value, and with yummy and mouth-watering taste?

Well, there aren’t many foods that can match these requirements better than waffles, smothered with honey or maple syrup. You do not need to whip eggs always to make your favourite waffle palatable, especially if you are a non-veg. There are great waffles which are absolutely eggless yet delicious. Here is how you can make fabulous eggless waffles with simple ingredients most likely you already have them on hand!

Ingredients


  1. 2 cups flour- alternatively whole wheat flour or mix
  2. 4 tsp baking powder
  3. 2 cups milk or 1 can of condensed milk diluted with water
  4. 2 tbsp sugar
  5. 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  6. 2 tbsp water
  7. ½ tsp vinegar
  8. 1 tsp vanilla
  9. 4 tbsp melted butter


Preparation


  1. Combine the dry ingredients - flour, baking powder and sugar.
  2. Mix together in a separate bowl the wet ingredients - milk, water, oil, vanilla, butter, and vinegar. Use of a whisk will help mixing better.
  3. Pour wet ingredient mix into the dry ingredients and mix together gently to make the batter thick yet flowing.
  4. Leave the batter in that condition for a few minutes to make it puffy.
  5. Preheat the waffle iron a little and grease it with butter.
  6. Scoop the batter in your waffle iron to fill its cavities and spread evenly. Heat the iron up for some time and get your waffles baked out Cook them a little longer, around 7-8 minutes, for crispy and golden brown edges. Use the remaining batter for more waffles.
  7. Serve waffles hot with honey, maple syrup, or chocolate sauce for toppings.


This is a simple waffle with vanilla flavor. You can add other flavorings as well such as cinnamon, nutella, cheese, cream,etc. Waffles usually are served with honey, jam, and fresh fruits.


Combination of baking soda and vinegar results in fluffiness of the waffle and makes for a good alternative of eggs. As the waffle comes out very fluffy and tasty, you just can’t make out the difference. Sufficient baking makes the waffles crispy and golden on the edges and airy, fluffy and spongy in the center.
