Eggless Charcoal Waffles

Black is mystic and the new craze for a waffle these days. Sexy charcoal waffles are making sensations with their unconventional aesthetics and health values. Charcoal is known for natural purification and is also used as a cooking ingredient - creating wonderful results with its ability to absorb organic toxins in the body. Additionally, charcoal powder absorbs moisture, making the charcoal waffles incredibly crisp over longer period of time.

Charcoal Waffles

Preparation time: 10 mins


Cooking time: 10 mins


Total time: 20 mins


Serves 4-5 people


Ingredients


  1. All purpose flour, sifted - 2.5 cups
  2. Almond milk, unsweetened or any other vegan milk - 2 cups
  3. Sugar - 4 tbsp
  4. Coconut oil - 4 tbsp
  5. Baking powder - 2 tsp
  6. Apple cider vinegar - 2 tsp
  7. Activated charcoal - 1 tsp
  8. Pure vanilla extract - 2 tsp
  9. 1 pinch of salt


Alternatively, you may use premix for eggless charcoal waffles which is a readymade blend of main ingredients. Add flour, sugar and milk as per the instruction given on the label.


Preparation


  1. Preheat the waffle iron and grease it with coconut oil.
  2. Combine the dry ingredients - Flour, sugar, and baking powder.
  3. Mix thoroughly in a separate bowl the wet ingredients - vegan milk, coconut oil, vanilla.
  4. Pour wet ingredient mix into the dry ingredients and stir it thoroughly until the batter is thick and flowing but not lumpy. Leave the batter in that condition to make it puffy.
  5. Scoop the batter in your waffle iron to fill its cavities and spread evenly. Heat the iron up for some time and get your waffles baked out Cook them a little longer, around 7-8 minutes, for crispy and golden brown edges.
  6. Serve waffles hot with coconut ice cream, maple syrup, or chocolate sauce for toppings or simply with white chocolate frosting.


This is a charcoal waffle with vanilla flavor. You can add other flavorings as well such as cinnamon, nutella, cheese, cream,etc. Combination of baking soda and vinegar results in fluffiness of the waffle and makes for a good alternative of eggs. As the waffle comes out very fluffy and tasty, you just can’t make out the difference. Sufficient baking makes the waffles crispy and golden on the edges and airy, fluffy and spongy in the center.
